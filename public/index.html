Headline

I've spent years helping teams at game jams with art, code, and music. And I kept seeing the same heartbreaking pattern with 3D games.

You'd have these brilliant, ambitious teams racing against the clock. They'd nail the concept art. Get the modeling and texturing done. But then they'd hit the wall: rigging and animation. Maybe they had one person who could model, but nobody who could rig. So the jam ends with a parade of T-pose characters sliding around the scene‚Äîlifeless mannequins in what could've been something special.

And then the legal scramble: frantically cobbling together licenses, trying to figure out if they can even release the game publicly. All that effort, and the game might never see the light of day because of licensing uncertainty.

But here's the thing about 3D that's actually magical: it's composable. That animal rig? Works on a dozen different creatures. That walk cycle you spent hours perfecting? Plug it into any biped character. The pipeline is incredibly powerful when you have the pieces to work with.

Mesh2Motion is the tool I wish existed at those game jams. Ready-to-use rigs and animations that work anywhere, with zero licensing headaches. So teams can spend their precious 48 hours building their vision‚Äînot wrestling with technical plumbing everyone needs but nobody wants to build from scratch.
